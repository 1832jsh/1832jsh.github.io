HP VXIbus Computers

What is VXI?

    VXI (VME Extensions for Instrumentation) is an interconnection bus used in test and measurement applications. VXI was originally conceived in 1987 by HP and several other test equipment manufacturers as a standard way that their products could connect together and replace the aging GPIB/HP-IB interface. Many of the key architectural elements of the VMEbus standard were carried over to VXI, such as the P1 connector pinout and standard module sizes. However, it should be noted that while mechanically compatible, VXI modules are NOT electrically compatible with VME systems and vice versa.

VXI Embedded Controllers

    HP Produced three models of non-x86 VXI embedded controllers, the first two, V/360 and V/382 were based upon the HP 9000 300 series of workstations using Motorola 68K series CPUs, specifically the model 360 and 382 respectively. The final model, V/743, was baseed upon the HP 9000 700 series workstations using PA-RISC CPUs, specifically the model 715. Later on HP would resell x86 based embedded controllers produced by Radisys, then a few models of x86 based embedded controllers developed in-house.
